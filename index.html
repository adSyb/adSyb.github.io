Kakamega Forest "Puzzli"

This project is based on the analog Kakamega Forest Puzzle "Puzzli" created by L. Paul in conjunction with her Master's thesis at the Hochschule Karlsruhe University of Applied Sciences. The purpose of this project was to create a browser-based version of the puzzle for use in the Kakamega Forest Information Center.

From the game tutorial:

"Just over 100 years ago, the Kakamega and Nandi Forests were connected and made up one large, forested area. Through studying old forest maps, aerial photographs taken from an airplane, and satellite imagery taken from space, we can see how the forest has changed over time.

To finish to game, fill the game area with all of the colored puzzle pieces representing the natural forest cover. The game will proceed in 3 rounds – one round for each time period – 2013, 1948/52, and 1912/13.

The goal of each round is to puzzle the pieces containing the colors for the respective year. The colors which should be puzzled during each round can be read from the color legend. For example, during the first round, 2013, the colors green, turquois and brown should be puzzled.

The pieces are located in the baskets below. After you place all the pieces for one round, you will be able to read about the forest cover durig that time period, and the reasons why some areas have lost or gained forest."
